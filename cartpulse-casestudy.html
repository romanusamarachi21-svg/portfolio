The CEO Brief

Revenue is growing. Profits are shrinking. Why?

CartPulse Nigeria — a B2C e-commerce platform selling Electronics, Fashion, and Household Goods across Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt — reported 18% revenue growth in FY2023. On paper, the business appeared to be having its best year. But behind the headlines, profitability was moving in the opposite direction. Sales were growing, yet the company wasn't becoming richer. The executive team was concerned: profits were not keeping pace.

As the Business Intelligence Analyst, I was tasked with answering four questions: Where are we losing money? Which customers are worth keeping? Why aren't our best-selling products making us richer? And which regions are actually most profitable?

My role was to investigate the data and uncover what the revenue figures were hiding

The Approach

A full-cycle BI workflow

1
Data Generation
Generated a realistic 20,200-row synthetic dataset using Python across Orders, Customers, and Products tables — with 11 intentional data quality issues built in.
2
Data Quality Assessment
Identified all 11 issues — missing values, duplicates, inconsistent formatting, typos, negative profits, and mixed date formats — and documented them in a formal Data Quality Report.
3
Data Cleaning in Excel
Applied TRIM, PROPER, SUBSTITUTE, nested IF, ABS, XLOOKUP, and Remove Duplicates to produce a clean, analysis-ready dataset — preserving the raw file throughout.
4
Business Intelligence Analysis
Analysed profitability by category, regional performance, product-level margins, and built RFM customer segmentation to identify high-value and at-risk customers.
5
Executive Power BI Dashboard
Built a 3-page interactive Power BI dashboard with DAX measures, KPI cards, slicers, and navigation — designed for executive decision making.

Key Findings

Four answers to the CEO's four questions

1. Revenue vs Profitability

All three categories sell roughly the same number of units (~6,600-6,700 orders each). Electronics dominates revenue and profit, but also costs the most to run and delivers the thinnest margins. The reason isn't volume-its high price points — averaging ₦79,375 per transaction. This makes us extremely sensitive to competitive price cuts.

2. Regional Performance

Port Harcourt leads in revenue and profit — but not because customers spend more. All three cities have virtually identical average order values (~₦141K) and profit margins (~44%). Port Harcourt simply has more orders: 7,433 vs Abuja's 6,394 and Lagos' 6,173. Lagos and Abuja are underleveraged markets.

3. Product Performance

The Prada Handbag delivers the highest absolute profit thanks to its premium price, even though its margin isn't the top performer. The Zara Midi Dress, however, is the true efficiency star- keeping 61.1% of every sale as profit. This tells us that high prices don't equal high efficiency and our most profitable products aren't always the ones with the biggest margins.

Recommendations

Five strategic actions for CartPulse's leadership team

1
Scale Fashion — especially Zara Midi Dress
With a 52.3% category margin and the Zara Midi Dress at 61.1%, Fashion is CartPulse's highest-efficiency category. Increasing Fashion sales volume is the fastest path to improving overall profitability without adding cost.
2
Review Electronics pricing — especially HP Laptop 15
All lowest-margin products are in Electronics. A pricing review focused on the HP Laptop 15 and similar high-cost items could significantly improve the category's contribution to overall profit.
3
Invest in Lagos and Abuja growth
Since all cities maintain equal margins and average order values, increasing order volume in Lagos and Abuja through targeted marketing would directly translate to proportional profit growth.
4
Protect Champions and Loyal Customers
The 547 Champions and Loyal customers are the backbone of CartPulse's revenue. A loyalty rewards programme or exclusive benefits would protect this critical segment from churn.
5
Launch an urgent At Risk win-back campaign
247 At Risk customers were once valuable but have gone quiet. A personalised win-back campaign with targeted discounts — launched before they move to Lost — could recover significant revenue.
